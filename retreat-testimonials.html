Life-changing trip (for the better)
Full disclosure... Natasha, guide and nature based medicine woman, is a family friend. I probably would not have made the trip down to Peru if not for knowing I'd be in her caring hands. I was even more delighted to witness the others, intially strangers but now friends, who also joined the excursion, feel the same way. 
My fellow travelers / companions and I always felt safe and in good hands; whether it was day jungle walks, day fishing, night walks, night boat outings or local village tours. The respect, love and overall knowledge Victor and Natasha have for Mother Nature, the jungle and all it has to offer is simply amazing to see. They truly understand that we humans have a symbiotic relationship with the jungle and therefore always made certain we gave our positive intentions and paid respect. Victor having grown up and spent most of his life in the jungle is well versed. I'm still amazed how he knows all the bird calls, other animal calls, and genus + species of much of the local flora. 
I was actually going to cancel my trip because I had fallen ill, but Natasha told me that the jungle and its natural medicines would heal me. While I arrived feeling horrible and weak, and somewhat skeptical; by the time I left, I felt the best I've been in 20 years. 
Advice: 
1) mosquitoes: bring mosquito repelling clothes. I had some but wish I had more; don't bring black colored clothes (mosquitoes apparently love black apparel); layer up (mosquitoes were able to bite me through my cotton tees); take B12 prior to coming down... apparently mosquitoes don't like it. Be careful with DEET - it's a neurotoxin and can hurt you too. If high concentration... Put on your clothes, not your skin... And do not touch wild life. 
2) intention: if going down to take natural meds whether it be kambo, ayuhuasca, etc... Don't go thinking it's recreational or to experiment with. Go with a specific goal(s) / intention(s) in mind; answers you seek or in which way you need healing (physical/mental/spiritual). Respect Mother Nature. Also do your own research ahead of time. ie. Pineal Gland decalcification; a good book is Infinite Mindfield by Anthony Peake; you'll appreciate the trip more. 
3) I'm vegan... And never ate better. Fresh veggies and fruit from the jungle was awesome; I was pleasantly surprised. 
4) No cell phone service: this may sound obvious but there is no cell / mobile phone coverage. Consider yourself warned; be ready to be unplugged and off the grid. *Your flashlight will be your best and most important asset. 
5) The jungle can be a dangerous place; but also awe-inspiring; life-changing. Be safe... More importantly have fun!!!
